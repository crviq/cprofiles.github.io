Mitski is a Japanese-American singer-songwriter known for her indie rock and indie folk music. Her full name is Mitski Miyawaki, and she was born on September 27, 1990, in Beppu, Japan. Mitski grew up moving between different countries due to her father's job as a geologist, spending time in Japan, Turkey, China, and the United States. She attended college in the United States, studying studio art and film, and began her music career while still in school.
Mitski gained critical acclaim with her early albums, including "Lush" (2012), "Be the Cowboy" (2018), and "Bury Me at Makeout Creek" (2014), which featured her introspective lyrics, raw vocals, and unique songwriting style. Her music often delves into themes of identity, loneliness, love, and mental health. Mitski has been praised for her candid and vulnerable songwriting, as well as her genre-blending approach that incorporates elements of rock, folk, punk, and pop.
Mitski has garnered a dedicated fanbase for her emotionally charged performances and her honest and relatable lyrics. She has received widespread critical acclaim for her music and has been nominated for and won several awards. Mitski has toured extensively and has gained a reputation as a captivating live performer. Her work has been influential in the indie rock and indie folk scenes, and she has been recognized for her contribution to the music industry as a woman of color and a representative of Asian-American artists.
